Stroke Risk Prediction — Non-linear Framingham Score

Machine Learning · Clinical Risk Modeling · Interpretability

  • Co-developed the Non-linear Framingham Stroke Risk Score (N-SRS) using Optimal Classification Trees on Framingham Heart Study data.
  • Integrated novel features (ECG markers, hematocrit, treatment status) and advanced imputation (MedImpute) to handle missing values.
  • Demonstrated significant improvement over the Revised FSRS: AUC ↑ to ~0.87 in derivation and ~0.74 in external validation cohorts.
  • Identified 23 unique stroke risk profiles, uncovering non-linear interactions (e.g. T-wave abnormalities, hematocrit levels, smoking impact).
  • Developed an interactive, interpretable tool for clinicians to personalize stroke prevention strategies.
