Stroke Risk Prediction — Non-linear Framingham Score

Machine Learning · Clinical Risk Modeling · Interpretability

  • Co-developed the Non-linear Framingham Stroke Risk Score (N-SRS) using Optimal Classification Trees on Framingham Heart Study data.
  • Integrated novel features (ECG markers, hematocrit, treatment status) and advanced imputation (MedImpute) to handle missing values.
  • Demonstrated significant improvement over the Revised FSRS: AUC ↑ to ~0.87 in derivation and ~0.74 in external validation cohorts.
  • Identified 23 unique stroke risk profiles, uncovering non-linear interactions (e.g. T-wave abnormalities, hematocrit levels, smoking impact).
  • Developed an interactive, interpretable tool for clinicians to personalize stroke prevention strategies.

Multidimensional Assays of Variant Effect in Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

Stanford · Genome Engineering · iPSC-CMs · Functional Genomics

  • Supported development of a scalable mapping strategy for >400 variants in the MYBPC3 gene, the major cause of hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.
  • Combined base editing at the native genomic locus with long-read RNA sequencing to assess splicing outcomes.
  • Integrated multidimensional assays in iPSC-derived cardiomyocytes: MYBPC3 abundance, hypertrophic signaling (BNP), and ubiquitin–proteasome function.
  • Identified splice-disrupting and missense variants, uncovering novel mechanisms beyond haploinsufficiency (e.g. proteasome impairment, structural instability).
  • Enabled reclassification of variants of uncertain significance (VUS) in clinically phenotyped HCM patients.
